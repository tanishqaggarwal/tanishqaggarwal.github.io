Honors and Awards

Pathfinding Autonomous Navigation (PAN)

A project under the Space Systems Design Studio headed by Dr. Mason Peck, PAN will launch two CubeSats into space on differing orbits and attempt to have them autonomously dock with one another. This project is the first known project to be attempting fully autonomous spacecraft rendezvous and docking manuevers in space. Find out more about the project here.

My job on this team is to engineer the control systems software and hardware that will be responsible for coordinating these manuevers. Using C++, I will be interfacing a Teensy 3.5, our central microcontroller, with the various system peripherals and sensors that will be installed on the spacecraft in order to determine the spacecraft's state.

Control Moment Gyroscope Polyhedral Rover

This was a former project under the Space Systems Design Studio headed by Dr. Mason Peck. Using the unique properties of control moment gyroscopes (CMGs), the goal of this project was to design and demonstrate the feasibility of a CMG-based rover.

I was scheduled to be the team lead of this project in Fall 2018. Unfortunately, due to funding and other constraints, this project was cancelled. While the team was still in existence, I was in charge of the electrical systems and software architecture, and was also responsible for coordinating design and development between the electrical and mechanical subteams.

Capture the Flag Tournaments (CTFs)

From 2014-2016, I was the captain of my high school's CTF competition team, Algorithmic Armadillos. CTFs are information security/hacking competitions where the objective is to obtain a string, called a flag, by cracking into cryptographic schemes or programs or websites. The team that collects the most number of strings out of a series of uniquely designed challenges wins the CTF tournament.

Our team placed well in several nationwide competitions, such as 3rd place in HSCTF 2015 and 2nd place in sCTF 2014.
